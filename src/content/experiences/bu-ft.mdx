At [Boston University's Wheelock Institute for the Science of Education (WISE)](https://www.wiseinstitute.net/about-us/), I play a key role in driving the technical development of three educational initiatives focusing on literacy interventions, game-based learning and generative AI integrations:

[National Center on Improving Literacy](https://improvingliteracy.org): Led a full redesign and platform migration from Wix to **WordPress**, working with stakeholders to develop a new visual identity in **Figma** before implementing it from scratch using the Bricks theme as the sole developer. I now handle ongoing maintenance and continue building out new content and functionality.

[NumberShire](https://www.numbershire.com/): Maintain and extend a **Node.js** backend supporting a Unity/WebGL math game, and manage a teacher and researcher dashboard that evaluates student gameplay to surface knowledge gaps and measure the impact of the games on learning. I deployed the inherited code for both services on a new **OpenShift** project using **Kubernetes** tooling.

[Chan Zuckerberg Initiative](https://chanzuckerberg.com/)-funded Research: Built a [web game prototype](https://reach-every-reader.onrender.com/games/irregular-word-reading/race-to-the-top) for pronunciation evaluation using **Azure** Speech Recognition to assess player responses in real-time. Currently developing infrastructure to aggregate data across multiple sources into a holistic view of student performance, with a focus on making those insights actionable through visualization and generative AI.
