At [Boston University's Wheelock Institute for the Science of Education (WISE)](https://www.wiseinstitute.net/about-us/), I contribute to three educational initiatives spanning literacy, game-based learning, and generative AI.

[National Center on Improving Literacy](https://improvingliteracy.org): As the sole developer, I led a full redesign and platform migration from Wix to **WordPress**, collaborating with stakeholders to develop a new visual identity in **Figma** before implementing it from scratch using the Bricks theme. I handle all ongoing maintenance and continue building out new content and functionality.

[NumberShire](https://www.numbershire.com/): As the sole backend developer, I maintain and extend a **Node.js** backend supporting a Unity/WebGL math game, and manage a teacher and researcher dashboard that surfaces student knowledge gaps and measures the impact of gameplay on learning outcomes. I also deployed both services on a new **OpenShift** project using **Kubernetes** tooling.

[Chan Zuckerberg Initiative](https://chanzuckerberg.com/)-funded Research: Built a [web game prototype](https://reach-every-reader.onrender.com/games/irregular-word-reading/race-to-the-top) for pronunciation evaluation using Azure Speech Recognition to assess player responses in real-time, and am currently working cross-team to architect infrastructure that aggregates data across multiple sources into a holistic view of student performance, with a focus on actionable insights through visualization and generative AI.
